# 🎣 Phishing Awareness Training

## 📝 Project Overview
[cite_start]This presentation is a comprehensive educational guide designed to train individuals on identifying and preventing **Phishing attacks**[cite: 60]. [cite_start]It breaks down the psychological tactics used by cybercriminals and provides actionable steps to maintain digital security[cite: 110, 120].

## 🚀 Key Learning Modules

### 1. Understanding Phishing
* [cite_start]**Definition:** A cyber attack used to steal sensitive data by pretending to be a trusted source[cite: 61, 62].
* [cite_start]**Targets:** Primarily aimed at obtaining passwords, banking details, and personal data[cite: 63].
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and unauthorized account access[cite: 66, 67, 68].

### 2. Common Attack Vectors
* [cite_start]**📧 Email Phishing:** Fake emails masquerading as banks or legitimate companies[cite: 71, 75].
* [cite_start]**📱 Smishing:** Phishing conducted via SMS text messages[cite: 72].
* [cite_start]**📞 Vishing:** Voice-based phishing over phone calls[cite: 73].
* [cite_start]**🎯 Spear Phishing:** Highly targeted attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations[cite: 74].

### 3. Identifying Red Flags
* [cite_start]**Urgency & Fear:** Messages that demand immediate action or threaten account suspension[cite: 81, 111, 112].
* [cite_start]**Suspicious Links:** URLs that look similar to real ones but are slightly altered, such as `http://bank-login123.com`[cite: 90, 96, 106].
* [cite_start]**Lack of Security:** Websites using **HTTP** instead of the secure **HTTPS** protocol[cite: 99, 107].
* [cite_start]**Generic Greetings:** Emails that use "Dear User" instead of a personalized name[cite: 87, 94].

---

## 🛡️ Prevention & Safe Browsing
* [cite_start]**Verify Senders:** Always check the actual email address of the sender, not just the display name[cite: 122].
* [cite_start]**Enable 2FA:** Use Two-Factor Authentication to add a second layer of security[cite: 125].
* [cite_start]**Check the Padlock:** Ensure the website has a valid SSL certificate (HTTPS 🔒) before entering data[cite: 124, 130].
* [cite_start]**Software Updates:** Keep antivirus and system software updated to defend against new threats[cite: 127, 128].

---

## 💡 Conclusion
[cite_start]Phishing remains a major threat because it manipulates human behavior rather than just software[cite: 110, 136]. [cite_start]The core message of this training is to **stay alert and think before you act**[cite: 139].
